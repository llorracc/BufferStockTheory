4The ‘natural’ constraint arises as a consequence of the budget constraint and the CRRA utility function which implies that the utility of consuming zero is negative inﬁnity. Its implications were ﬁrst explored by Zeldes (1989) in a life cycle context. Carroll (1992) analyzed the inﬁnite horizon case, and Aiyagari (1994) coined the term ‘natural borrowing constraint.’
